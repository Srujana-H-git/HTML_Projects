ALL ABOUT ROCKETS

There are two types of Rockets or launch vehicles they are
(1) PSLV
(2) GSLV

Polar satellite launch vehicles (PSLV)


The Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) is India's workhorse launch vehicle developed by ISRO. Renowned for its versatility and reliability, PSLV has successfully launched satellites into polar, geostationary, and sun-synchronous orbits. It supports diverse missions, from remote sensing to interplanetary exploration, and played a pivotal role in India's space achievements like Chandrayaan and Mangalyaan.

Geo-synchronous satellite Launch vehicles (GSLV)


The Geosynchronous Satellite Launch Vehicle (GSLV) is a key launch vehicle developed by ISRO for deploying heavier payloads into geostationary orbits. Equipped with a powerful cryogenic upper stage, GSLV enables India to launch advanced communication and weather satellites. It supports strategic missions, including Chandrayaan-2, and strengthens India's self-reliance in space technology.
